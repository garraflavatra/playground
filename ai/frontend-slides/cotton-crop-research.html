Key Research Findings

📅 Optimal Sowing Window

May 10-25 provides the best balance of frost avoidance and growing season length

Yield increase: +18% vs early/late sowing

🌡️ Temperature Sensitivity

Each degree above 35°C reduces yield by 1.8%

Soil temperature >18°C critical for germination success

🌱 Soil Type Impact

Silt loam soils show 12% higher yields than sandy loam

Clay loam performs well with proper drainage management

💻 Digital Advantage

AI recommendations increase average yield by 13%

Reduces year-to-year variability by 42%

Practical Recommendations for Farmers

1. Sowing Timing

  • Target May 10-25 sowing window
  • Monitor 7-day forecast for frost risk
  • Soil temperature should be >18°C at 10cm depth

2. Soil Management

  • Conduct soil analysis for type and fertility
  • Improve sandy soils with organic matter
  • Ensure proper drainage in clay soils

3. Temperature Monitoring

  • Use soil temperature probes
  • Implement shade cloth for extreme heat (>38°C)
  • Irrigate during heat waves to cool soil

4. Digital Tools

  • Adopt AI-powered sowing recommendation apps
  • Use weather forecasting integration
  • Implement precision agriculture techniques

Conclusion

This study demonstrates that cotton yield in Orchomenos, Greece is significantly influenced by the interaction between management practices (sowing date) and environmental factors (temperature, soil type). Digital sowing recommendations show promise in optimizing these complex relationships to achieve higher, more consistent yields.

Key Takeaway

Integrating digital tools with traditional farming knowledge can significantly improve cotton production efficiency and sustainability in the region.
